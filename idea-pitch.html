ABOUT IDEA Pitch

For two minutes

Two minutes, sounds easy, right? Who can't talk for two minutes?
It'll be the hardest two minutes of your month. To summarize your company's value proposition in an interesting way is a challenge for many seasoned entrepreneurs. But that's what IDEA Pitch asks you to do. You won't regret having a quality elevator pitch, so why not commit to perfecting one?

For assessment

We're very proud of the panelists that we have to offer, as they represent some of the best mentors available in Charlotte. If you really want your idea assessed, though, the IDEA Fund Partners do this professionally. Their companies have had combined exits of over $1 billion dollars, so you know they are not going to make things easy on you.

For growth

The experience of pitching at TechBreakfast CLT is focused on getting practice in front of our positive, supportive community. Our best pitchsters, however, will have bigger opportunities than making friends while drinking free juice. Unlike TechBreakfast, if a presenter nails their pitch and the Q+A session to follow, the IDEA Fund Partners may bring you to Durham to do your pitch for the assembled investment team.

Chris Halligan

Chris Halligan is an experienced, successful technology executive and entrepreneur.

Upon graduation from the University of Arizona in Tucson in 1989, he started his career with more than 10 years at Dell Computer Corporation in Austin, Texas and Tokyo, Japan. During Mr. Halligan's time at Dell, he was the company's Salesman of the Year in 1991 and then held several sales and marketing management positions, culminating in responsibility for over $2B per year in commercial revenues conducted over the Internet in 1999.

Following Dell, Mr. Halligan ran North America for webMethods during a period highlighted by explosive revenue growth and the firm's IPO - one of the most successful in software history. He then became part owner of Mascot Books, a leader in the self publishing space for children's authors and co-founded Kieden, a software startup which was purchased by salesforce.com in 2006.

Next, Mr. Halligan became Chief Executive Officer for PokerTek (nasdaq: ptek), a Matthews, NC based gaming company and the world leader in automated poker technology. During his tenure, PokerTek grew from $4M to $13M in annual revenues, while reducing company operating expenses by more than 10%.

His next startup, OtherScreen, can teach you something about failure.

He is now Operating Partner at IDEA Fund. He is married to Sarah and has three cool kids who attend Providence Day School in Charlotte.
